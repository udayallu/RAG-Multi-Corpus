Best Ways to Use Your Car AC in All Seasons

For Aventro Motors Car Owners

Your vehicle’s air conditioning (AC) system is vital for comfort and safety throughout the year. Whether it’s cooling in summer, defogging in monsoon, or maintaining a pleasant environment in winter, knowing how to use the AC efficiently will extend system life and enhance your driving experience. Here’s a guide to making the most of your AC system during every season.

Summer: Beat the Heat

1. Ventilate First
- Before switching on the AC, roll down the windows and drive for a couple of minutes to expel hot air.
- This makes the cabin cool down faster.

2. Use Recirculation Mode
- Turn on the AC and set it to “recirculate” once the cabin is cool. - This prevents hot outside air from entering, making the system more effective.

3. Set the Right Temperature
- Avoid setting the thermostat to the lowest setting. 22-24°C (72-75°F) is usually comfortable and easier on the compressor.

4. Regular Maintenance
- Clean or replace the cabin air filter. - Get your AC system inspected and refrigerant topped up at an Aventro Service Center before summer starts.

Monsoon: Fight Humidity and Fog

1. Use AC for Defogging
- Turn on the AC with the blower directed at the windshield to quickly clear fog. - Use the “fresh air” mode for a few minutes to reduce internal humidity.

2. Don’t Use Recirculate Mode Continuously
- Constant recirculation during rains may increase humidity inside, causing more fogging.

3. Keep Windows Closed
- Prevent water and more humidity from entering the cabin.

Winter: Comfort with Care

1. Run the AC Regularly
- Turn on the AC for at least 10 minutes once a week, even in winter.
- This keeps seals lubricated and prevents mold in the system.

2. Use AC with Heater for Defogging
- Warm air alone may not clear the windshield.
- Use the AC and heater together (AC dries the air, heater warms it) for effective demisting.

General Tips for All Seasons

  • Service Regularly: Schedule professional AC checks at Aventro Service Centers.
  • Check for Odors: Foul smells may indicate mold – get the filters and system checked.
  • Avoid Overloading: Don’t overload your car; heavy loads reduce AC efficiency.
  • Park in Shade: Protect your car from direct sun to reduce AC workload.

When to Seek Professional Help

  • AC not cooling/heating as expected
  • Unusual noises/smells from the vents
  • Visible leaks under your car
