How to Start Your Aventro Car

Starting your Aventro car is a simple process, but exact procedures may vary slightly depending on the model, fuel type, and technology (conventional, electric, or hybrid). Below is a general step-by-step guide for Aventro Petrol, Diesel, Hybrid, and Electric cars.

For All Aventro Models

1. Ensure Safety First

  • Sit in the driver’s seat and fasten your seatbelt.
  • Ensure the gear lever is in ‘P’ (Park, for automatic) or ‘N’ (Neutral, for manual).
  • Press the brake pedal (and the clutch for manual transmission).

A. Petrol & Diesel Models

With Standard Key Ignition

  1. Insert the Key:
    • Insert the car key into the ignition slot (usually on the right of the steering column).
  2. Turn the Key:
    • Turn the key clockwise to the “ON” position (dashboard lights turn on).
    • Continue turning to the “START” position. Release when the engine starts.

With Push-Button Start

  1. Ensure Key Fob is Inside the Car:
    • The key fob should be in your pocket or bag within the vehicle.
  2. Press the Start/Stop Button:
    • Press and hold the brake pedal.
    • Press the “Start/Stop” button. The engine will start.

B. Electric Models

  1. Key Fob Present
    • Ensure the key fob is inside the vehicle.
  2. Press Brake Pedal
    • Firmly press and hold the brake pedal.
  3. Press Start Button
    • Press the “Start/Stop” button. Indicator lights and systems will activate.
  4. Ready Indicator
    • Wait for the ‘READY’ signal/light on the dashboard. The car is now ready to drive silently.

C. Hybrid Models

  1. Key Fob Present
    • Make sure the key fob is with you, inside the car.
  2. Press Brake Pedal
    • Press and hold the brake pedal.
  3. Press Start Button
    • Press the “Start/Stop” button.
    • The car may start silently in EV mode or with a slight engine sound in engine mode. Watch for ‘READY’ signal on the dashboard.

Additional Tips

  • For all models, ensure all warning lights turn off after starting.
  • Do not press the accelerator while starting.
  • For electric/hybrid, if ‘READY’ does not appear, try restarting.

If you’re unsure or the car doesn’t start, refer to your Aventro user manual or contact the nearest Aventro Service Center.
