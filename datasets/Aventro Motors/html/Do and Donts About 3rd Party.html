Do’s and Don’ts About 3rd Party Accessories & Electrical Accessories for Your Car

When it comes to enhancing your Aventro vehicle with aftermarket or third-party accessories, especially electrical ones, it’s critical to protect your investment, warranty, and safety. Here’s a comprehensive guide outlining the essential do’s and don’ts.

Do’s

1. Do Research Thoroughly

  • Always check the compatibility of the accessory with your specific Aventro model and variant.
  • Look for genuine reviews and ratings from other users and professionals.

2. Do Buy from Reputed Dealers

  • Purchase accessories, especially electrical ones, from trusted and reputed vendors.
  • Ensure that the accessory comes with proper certification and warranty.

3. Do Consult Aventro Authorized Service

  • Before installation, consult your nearest Aventro Service Center or authorized dealer for advice.
  • Get their approval for high-voltage or safety-critical electrical changes (e.g., battery packs, infotainment systems).

4. Do Verify Warranty Implications

  • Ask specifically about warranty impact: certified installations at Aventro Service Centers are less likely to void your warranty.

5. Do Ensure Professional Installation

  • Have all accessories, particularly electrical or electronic ones, fitted by trained technicians.
  • Use proper connectors, fuses, and avoid splicing into factory wiring harnesses.

6. Do Prioritize Safety Standards

  • Only use accessories compliant with BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) and other safety norms.
  • For electrical parts, ensure proper ratings for voltage and current.

7. Do Retain All Purchase and Installation Records

  • Keep invoices, product manuals, and warranties safe.
  • Maintain a record of all modifications and installations for future reference.

Don’ts

1. Don’t Compromise Vehicle Warranty

  • Installing uncertified or incompatible accessories can void your company warranty—consult Aventro first!
  • Never bypass or disable factory safety features (like airbags, sensors, anti-theft systems).

2. Don’t Use Low-Cost, Sub-Standard Parts

  • Avoid cheap, uncertified or counterfeit electrical accessories—they can cause malfunction, fire, or permanent damage.

3. Don’t Overload the Electrical System

  • Never install accessories (high-powered audio, extra lights, etc.) that draw excessive power without proper upgrades (e.g., alternator, wiring).

4. Don’t Attempt DIY Installation for Complex Accessories

  • Never attempt to install complex electrical or electronic components yourself unless you’re certified.
  • Avoid modifying or cutting factory wires, as this can cause electrical faults.

5. Don’t Ignore Warning Signs After Installation

  • If you notice dashboard warnings, flickering lights, or malfunctioning electronics post-installation, stop using the accessory immediately and consult a service center.

6. Don’t Block Airbags or Sensors

  • Never install accessories (phone holders, seat covers, dashboards gadgets) that block airbags, seat belt pre-tensioners, or sensors.

7. Don’t Forget Insurance Implications

  • Inform your insurer if you add any expensive or electrical accessories—undisclosed modifications may impact future claims.
