# Cendara University Entrance Exam Information

---

## Overview

Cendara University administers entrance examinations for select undergraduate and graduate academic programs to ensure a high standard of academic readiness and to appropriately place students in courses aligned with their skill levels. While most degree programs do not require a standardized university-wide entrance exam, certain competitive majors, specialized scholarship pathways, and direct-entry professional tracks require subject-based or aptitude testing.

Students are encouraged to review this section thoroughly and consult with their academic advisor or the Office of Admissions to determine whether an entrance exam applies to their intended course of study.

---

## Entrance Exam Requirements

### Which Programs Require Entrance Exams?

- **School of Health Sciences**  
  *Bachelor of Science in Nursing, Bachelor of Public Health*  
  - Science Aptitude Exam (required for all first-time applicants)
- **School of Engineering and Applied Sciences**  
  *Bachelor of Engineering, Computer Science Major*  
  - Mathematics Proficiency Exam (required for all direct-entry applicants)
- **Honors College**  
  - Critical Reasoning Essay (for applicants seeking admission to Honors College)
- **Graduate School**  
  - Program-specific subject exams as required by departmental guidelines (e.g., Psychology GRE Subject for MA in Psychology)

**Note:** General undergraduate admission does **not** require a university-wide entrance exam; most applicants are evaluated using prior academic transcripts, standardized testing (SAT/ACT), and supplemental materials.

## Exam Registration Process

### 1. Determine Exam Eligibility

- Refer to your program page in the [Online Program Catalog](https://catalog.cendara.edu) or consult with your assigned admissions counselor.
- Check for exemption criteria (e.g., transfer of equivalent coursework, qualifying national/international test scores).

### 2. Register for the Exam

- Log in to MyCendara at [my.cendara.edu](https://my.cendara.edu).
- Navigate to the “Entrance Exams” section under the Applicant Dashboard.
- Select your required exam(s) and preferred examination session.
- Review and confirm testing accommodations if applicable (see Accessibility Services below).

### 3. Confirm Exam Schedule

- Once registered, you will receive an email within 48 hours containing:
    - Exam date and time
    - Testing location or access link (for online proctored exams)
    - Required identification and materials
- Exam schedules are published at [cendara.edu/entrance-exams/schedule](https://cendara.edu/entrance-exams/schedule).

## Exam Content and Format

### Science Aptitude Exam

- **Duration:** 90 minutes
- **Sections:**  
  - Biology basics (30%)
  - Chemistry fundamentals (35%)
  - Quantitative reasoning (25%)
  - Scientific reading comprehension (10%)
- **Question Types:** Multiple-choice and short answer

### Mathematics Proficiency Exam

- **Duration:** 75 minutes
- **Sections:**  
  - Algebra and functions (40%)
  - Trigonometry and geometry (30%)
  - Data analysis and interpretation (30%)
- **Question Types:** Computer-adaptive multiple-choice
  
### Critical Reasoning Essay

- **Duration:** 45 minutes
- **Format:** Typed essay response to one prompt
- **Evaluation:** Clarity of argument, logic, use of evidence, and writing mechanics

### Graduate Subject Exams

- **Content and format** are determined by each department; see graduate program entry in the [Catalog](https://catalog.cendara.edu/graduate).

## Scoring and Results

- **Exams are scored within 10 business days.**
    - Undergraduate exam scores are posted to your MyCendara Applicant Dashboard.
    - Graduate subject exam results are communicated by program departments.
- **Minimum Passing Scores:**
    - Science Aptitude Exam: 68% overall, no section below 55%
    - Mathematics Proficiency Exam: 65%
    - Critical Reasoning Essay: Score of 8/12 from faculty readers
- **Retake Policy:**  
  Students who do not meet minimum scores may register for one exam retake per admission cycle. Requests for additional attempts require appeal to the Admissions Review Committee.

## Exam Day Guidelines

- Arrive at least 30 minutes before your scheduled start time.
- Bring a government-issued photo ID and your CUSID exam ticket (print or digital).
- Permitted items:  
  - Non-programmable calculator (for Math exam)
  - Pencils/pens (where applicable)
  - Water in clear bottle
- Prohibited items:  
  - Study aids, notes, or electronic devices (other than those required for online testing)
  - Wearable technology

Accommodations for students with disabilities are available. Contact accessibility@cendara.edu at least two weeks before your scheduled exam.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do all applicants need to take an entrance exam?

No. Only certain academic programs or tracks require an entrance exam (see above). Most applicants qualify through their prior academic record and submitted standardized scores.

### What if I am unable to attend the scheduled exam date?

Contact the Office of Admissions at exams@cendara.edu as soon as possible. Make-up sessions or remote testing may be arranged for verifiable reasons such as illness, travel difficulties, or overlapping commitments.

### How should I prepare for my entrance exam?

- Review the sample test materials and practice guides available at [cendara.edu/entrance-exams/prep](https://cendara.edu/entrance-exams/prep).
- Attend an online information session or contact your department for recommended resources.

### Can international students take exams online?

Yes. Online, proctored versions of all entrance exams are available for international and remote applicants. Ensure your technology meets the system requirements published on the website.

### Are there exam fees?

The first sitting of any required entrance exam is included with your application. Retake or late registration fees are $45 per exam. Fee waivers are available for eligible students (see [Financial Services](mailto:finance@cendara.edu)).
