# Cendara University Course Catalog  
### Department of Curriculum & Instruction

---

## Undergraduate Courses  
### Bachelor of Education (B.Ed.) in Elementary Education

---

#### **EDUC 1101: Foundations of Education**
- **Description:**  
  Introduction to the philosophy, history, and social context of elementary education. Topics include educational theories, current issues in teaching, and the role of educators in diverse classrooms.
- **Prerequisites:** None  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDUC 1202: Child Development and Learning**
- **Description:**  
  Examination of key physical, cognitive, and social-emotional development stages in children aged 4–12. Explores theories of learning and their application in diverse elementary classrooms.
- **Prerequisites:** None  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDUC 1303: Curriculum Design for Elementary Classrooms**
- **Description:**  
  Study of principles and models of curriculum development. Students will develop lesson plans and curricular units aligned with state and national standards.
- **Prerequisites:** EDUC 1101  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDUC 1404: Literacy Instruction I**
- **Description:**  
  Approaches to teaching reading and writing from kindergarten through third grade. Emphasizes phonemic awareness, phonics, and early literacy strategies.  
- **Prerequisites:** EDUC 1202  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDUC 1505: Mathematics for Elementary Teachers**
- **Description:**  
  Content and methods for effective mathematics instruction at the elementary level, including numeracy, problem-solving, and assessment techniques.
- **Prerequisites:** EDUC 1101  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDUC 2106: Classroom Assessment and Evaluation**
- **Description:**  
  Principles and practices of student assessment, including formative, summative, and alternative forms. Focus on data-driven instruction to improve student outcomes.
- **Prerequisites:** EDUC 1303  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDUC 2207: Literacy Instruction II**
- **Description:**  
  Strategies for reading comprehension and writing instruction, grades 4–6. Focuses also on differentiated instruction for students with varying abilities.
- **Prerequisites:** EDUC 1404  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDUC 2308: Science and Social Studies Methods**
- **Description:**  
  Integration of science and social studies content and pedagogy, with an emphasis on inquiry-based learning and interdisciplinary instruction.
- **Prerequisites:** EDUC 1303  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDUC 3109: Inclusive Classrooms and Special Needs Education**
- **Description:**  
  Methods for supporting diverse learners, including those with exceptionalities and English language learners. Legal and ethical aspects of inclusion addressed.
- **Prerequisites:** EDUC 2106  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDUC 3110: Classroom Management**
- **Description:**  
  Techniques for creating positive learning environments, managing student behavior, and implementing preventative strategies.
- **Prerequisites:** EDUC 2106  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDUC 4201: Student Teaching Seminar**
- **Description:**  
  Capstone seminar to support students completing their student teaching experience. Emphasizes reflective practice, professional development, and portfolio creation.
- **Prerequisites:** Completion of all prior B.Ed. coursework; approval of Department Chair  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDUC 4202: Student Teaching Practicum**
- **Description:**  
  Supervised full-time teaching placement in a local elementary school. Students apply pedagogical theories in real-world settings.
- **Prerequisites:** EDUC 4201  
- **Credit Hours:** 6  

---

## Graduate Courses  
### Master of Education (M.Ed.) in Curriculum & Instruction

---

#### **EDCI 5001: Contemporary Issues in Curriculum & Instruction**
- **Description:**  
  Exploration of current trends, reform movements, and policy debates influencing curriculum and instructional practice in K-12 education.
- **Prerequisites:** Admission to M.Ed. program  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDCI 5102: Advanced Instructional Theory**
- **Description:**  
  In-depth analysis of learning theories, instructional models, and evidence-based practices for enhancing student achievement across grade levels.
- **Prerequisites:** EDCI 5001  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDCI 5203: Curriculum Development and Evaluation**
- **Description:**  
  Study of systematic approaches to designing, implementing, and assessing curricula using quantitative and qualitative methods.
- **Prerequisites:** EDCI 5001  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDCI 5304: Educational Research Methods**
- **Description:**  
  Introduction to qualitative and quantitative educational research methodologies, including data collection, analysis, and interpretation for classroom settings.
- **Prerequisites:** None  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDCI 5405: Technology Integration in the Classroom**
- **Description:**  
  Strategies for selecting and implementing digital tools and resources to enhance teaching and learning. Includes evaluation of emerging educational technologies.
- **Prerequisites:** None  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDCI 6106: Cultural Competence and Equity in Education**
- **Description:**  
  Examination of approaches to fostering inclusive and equitable learning environments. Topics include anti-bias education and culturally responsive pedagogy.
- **Prerequisites:** EDCI 5001  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDCI 6207: Leadership in Curriculum & Instruction**
- **Description:**  
  Training in instructional leadership, including supervision, mentoring, and professional development for teachers.
- **Prerequisites:** EDCI 5203  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDCI 6308: Thesis Seminar**
- **Description:**  
  Guided support for developing and completing a scholarly research thesis in the field of curriculum and instruction.
- **Prerequisites:** EDCI 5304; approval of Thesis Advisor  
- **Credit Hours:** 6  

---

#### **EDCI 6309: Capstone Project**
- **Description:**  
  Applied research project addressing a practical problem in curriculum and instruction. Students present findings to faculty and peers.
- **Prerequisites:** EDCI 5304  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

## Elective Courses

---

#### **EDCI 6501: Differentiated Instruction Strategies**
- **Description:**  
  Methods for tailoring instruction to meet the needs of diverse learners, including gifted students and those with learning differences.
- **Prerequisites:** For B.Ed.: EDUC 3109; For M.Ed.: None  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDCI 6502: Assessment Tools and Data Analysis**
- **Description:**  
  Advanced techniques for designing, administering, and interpreting classroom assessments, with an emphasis on data-driven instructional decision-making.
- **Prerequisites:** EDUC 2106 or EDCI 5304  
- **Credit Hours:** 3  

---

#### **EDCI 6503: Global Perspectives in Education**
- **Description:**  
  Comparative analysis of international education systems, policies, and practices. Focus on global challenges and innovations in curriculum.
- **Prerequisites:** None  
- **Credit Hours:** 3
