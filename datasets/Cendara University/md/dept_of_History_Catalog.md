# Cendara University  
## Department of History  
### Course Catalog 2024–2025

---

## Undergraduate Courses (B.A. in History)

---

### HIST 101: Foundations of World History  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
An introductory survey of global historical developments from ancient civilizations to the early modern era. Topics include early societies, major world religions, trade networks, empires, and cross-cultural encounters.  
**Prerequisites:** None

---

### HIST 115: Introduction to Historical Methods  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
A practical course exploring how historians investigate and interpret the past. Students gain experience with primary sources, historical arguments, and bibliography construction. Includes workshops on archival research and oral history interviews.  
**Prerequisites:** None

---

### HIST 201: History of the United States to 1877  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
A comprehensive overview of American history from pre-colonial societies through Reconstruction. Focus on colonial life, the American Revolution, Constitution, Civil War, and post-war transformations.  
**Prerequisites:** None

---

### HIST 213: Modern European History, 1789–Present  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
An analysis of major political, social, and cultural developments in Europe since the French Revolution. Topics include nationalism, industrialization, the world wars, the Cold War, and the European Union.  
**Prerequisites:** HIST 101 or consent of instructor

---

### HIST 225: History of East Asia  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
An introduction to the histories of China, Japan, and Korea from ancient times to the 20th century. Emphasis on changing political structures, cultural exchanges, and regional conflicts.  
**Prerequisites:** None

---

### HIST 320: Public History: Theory and Practice  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
This course explores the theory and practice of public history, including museum curation, historic preservation, and community history projects. Students participate in local public history initiatives and develop professional portfolios.  
**Prerequisites:** HIST 115

---

### HIST 330: Gender in World History  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
A comparative study of gender roles and identities in various global societies from antiquity to the present. Includes examinations of family, labor, law, and cultural representations.  
**Prerequisites:** One 200-level HIST course

---

### HIST 350: The Historian’s Craft  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
Advanced instruction in historiographical debates, interpretation of evidence, and historical writing. Culminates in a major research paper using primary and secondary sources.  
**Prerequisites:** HIST 115 and at least one 200-level HIST course

---

### HIST 390: Special Topics in History  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
Seminar on selected themes or regions of historical inquiry, varying by semester (e.g., Migration in the Ancient World, African Kingdoms, Environmental History).  
**Prerequisites:** At least one 300-level HIST course or consent of instructor

---

### HIST 495: Senior Seminar in History  
**Credit Hours:** 4  
**Description:**  
Capstone course featuring advanced research, seminar discussion, and the preparation of a substantial thesis. Required for all senior majors.  
**Prerequisites:** HIST 350 and senior standing

---

## Graduate Courses (M.A./Ph.D. in History)

---

### HIST 505: Graduate Historiography  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
Intensive survey of historiographical traditions and theoretical approaches in historical scholarship. Students examine the evolution of historical writing, methodological debates, and contemporary trends in the discipline.  
**Prerequisites:** Admission to graduate program in History

---

### HIST 511: Archival Research Methods  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
Hands-on training in archival theory, paleography, and digital archival tools. Includes fieldwork at the Cendara University Special Collections and local archives.  
**Prerequisites:** HIST 505

---

### HIST 521: Seminar in Early Modern History  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
Graduate-level analysis of key issues in early modern global history. Emphasis on comparative empire studies, religious reforms, and scientific revolutions.  
**Prerequisites:** HIST 505 or consent of instructor

---

### HIST 532: Topics in American Historiography  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
Explores major topics and methodological innovations in the writing of American history, with critical evaluation of primary and secondary sources.  
**Prerequisites:** HIST 505

---

### HIST 545: Digital History and Public Engagement  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
This course introduces digital tools for historical research and public presentation, including GIS mapping, digital exhibition design, and social media outreach. Students develop and present collaborative digital projects.  
**Prerequisites:** HIST 511

---

### HIST 587: History and Memory  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Description:**  
An interdisciplinary seminar on the uses of memory in shaping historical narratives, including studies of commemoration, collective memory, and the politics of remembrance.  
**Prerequisites:** HIST 505

---

### HIST 610: Advanced Research Seminar  
**Credit Hours:** 4  
**Description:**  
Designed for students preparing thesis or dissertation projects. Students present their research, receive critiques, and engage in in-depth methodological discussions.  
**Prerequisites:** 18 graduate credit hours in History, including HIST 505 and HIST 511

---

### HIST 699: Dissertation Research  
**Credit Hours:** Variable (1–9)  
**Description:**  
Independent research under the supervision of a faculty advisor leading to the doctoral dissertation. May be repeated for credit.  
**Prerequisites:** Admission to candidacy for Ph.D. in History

---
