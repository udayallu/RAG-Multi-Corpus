Course Catalog 2024-2025

Undergraduate Courses

PSY 101: Introduction to Psychology

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    An overview of the major theories, foundational concepts, and methods in psychology. Topics include perception, learning, motivation, development, and social behavior. Designed for students with no prior background in psychology.
  • Prerequisites: None

PSY 110: Research Methods in Psychology

  • Credits: 4
  • Description:
    Introduction to the scientific method as applied to psychological phenomena. Students learn experimental design, data collection, basic analysis, and ethical considerations. Includes laboratory sessions.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 101

PSY 211: Developmental Psychology

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    Examination of the biological, cognitive, and social development of individuals from infancy through adulthood. Explores key theories and methodological approaches.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 101

PSY 218: Introduction to Cognitive Psychology

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    Exploration of mental processes such as attention, memory, language, perception, and problem-solving. Discussion of experimental findings and theoretical frameworks.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 101

PSY 230: Social Psychology

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    Investigation of how social factors influence individual behavior. Major topics include aggression, conformity, group dynamics, attitudes, stereotypes, and prejudice.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 101

PSY 250: Abnormal Psychology

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    Analysis of the definition, classification, and treatment of mental disorders. Includes an overview of anxiety, mood, psychotic, and personality disorders.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 101

PSY 312: Biological Bases of Behavior

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    Study of the neural and hormonal foundations of behavior, sensation, and motivation. Emphasizes connections between biological mechanisms and psychological processes.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 101, PSY 110

PSY 320: Introduction to Clinical Psychology

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    Survey of the principles and practices of clinical assessment, diagnosis, and treatment of mental health conditions. Emphasis on evidence-based approaches.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 250

PSY 340: Personality Psychology

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    Exploration of classical and contemporary theories of personality, methods of assessment, and research findings.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 101

PSY 370: Psychological Statistics

  • Credits: 4
  • Description:
    Introduction to descriptive and inferential statistical techniques used in psychological research, including hypothesis testing, ANOVA, regression, and nonparametric approaches. Includes computer-based labs.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 110, MTH 101 (Introduction to College Algebra)

PSY 399: Independent Study in Psychology

  • Credits: 1-3 (variable)
  • Description:
    Individual study or research project under faculty supervision in a specialized area of psychology. Requires departmental approval.
  • Prerequisites: Permission of instructor, Junior standing

Graduate Courses

PSY 500: Advanced Research Methods

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    In-depth examination of quantitative and qualitative research methodologies, data analysis techniques, and ethics in advanced psychological research.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 370 or equivalent

PSY 512: Cognitive Neuroscience

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    Study of neural mechanisms underlying cognitive functions such as perception, memory, and decision-making. Readings from current research literature.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 312

PSY 522: Advanced Developmental Psychology

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    Critical analysis of cognitive and emotional development throughout the lifespan. Focus on current issues and empirical research.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 211

PSY 532: Psychopathology

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    Advanced exploration of psychological disorders, diagnostic criteria, underlying mechanisms, and recent research trends.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 250, PSY 320

PSY 540: Psychological Assessment

  • Credits: 4
  • Description:
    Principles and practices of assessment in clinical psychology, including intelligence, personality, and neuropsychological testing.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 340, PSY 320

PSY 550: Interventions in Clinical Psychology

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    Comprehensive overview of psychotherapeutic interventions, including cognitive-behavioral, psychodynamic, and humanistic approaches. Integrates case studies and supervised practice.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 532, PSY 540

PSY 561: Seminar in Social Cognition

  • Credits: 2
  • Description:
    Discussion-based seminar focusing on theory and research in social cognitive processes including attitudes, attribution, and emotion regulation.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 230 (or equivalent)

PSY 580: Teaching of Psychology

  • Credits: 2
  • Description:
    Preparation for teaching undergraduate psychology, covering course planning, assessment, and student engagement strategies. Required for teaching assistants.
  • Prerequisites: Graduate standing in Psychology

PSY 590: Graduate Thesis

  • Credits: 6
  • Description:
    Original empirical research conducted under faculty mentorship. Culminates in a thesis and oral defense.
  • Prerequisites: Permission of thesis committee

Doctoral-Level Courses

PSY 601: Advanced Topics in Clinical Psychology

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    Rotating content seminar on topics such as trauma, substance use disorders, and multicultural assessment. Students critically evaluate recent interventions and research.
  • Prerequisites: Admission to Ph.D.Â program

PSY 610: Statistical Modeling for Psychologists

  • Credits: 3
  • Description:
    Intensive training in advanced statistical techniques including multilevel modeling, structural equation modeling, and Bayesian estimation.
  • Prerequisites: PSY 500

PSY 635: Grant Writing in Psychology

  • Credits: 2
  • Description:
    Practical experience in identifying funding sources and developing research grant proposals with feedback from faculty and peers.
  • Prerequisites: Graduate standing

PSY 650: Doctoral Dissertation

  • Credits: 12
  • Description:
    Completion of an original dissertation project demonstrating substantial contribution to the field of psychology. Includes proposal, data collection and analysis, manuscript preparation, and defense.
  • Prerequisites: Advancement to candidacy

Additional Notes

  • All courses are subject to availability and may require consent from instructors or the department.
  • Undergraduate students interested in graduate coursework must obtain special permission.
  • Credit hours are based on semester system.
