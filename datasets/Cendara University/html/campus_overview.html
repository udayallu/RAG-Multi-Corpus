Cendara University Campus Overview

Introduction

Founded in 1972, Cendara University spans a 180-acre campus nestled on the eastern edge of Windham City. The campus combines modern innovation with a respect for natural landscapes, offering an environment conducive to academic excellence and community engagement.

Physical Description of Facilities

Academic Buildings

  • Morgan Academic Complex
    Constructed: 1974, Renovated 2017
    Description: Three interconnected wings house 60 classrooms, 7 computer labs, and 15 faculty offices. The complex supports the College of Arts & Humanities and features multimedia-enabled lecture halls (maximum capacity: 250 students each).

  • Rosenfeld Science Center
    Constructed: 1983, Expanded 2015
    Description: A four-story structure dedicated to the Departments of Biology, Chemistry, and Environmental Sciences. Facilities include:

    • 8 research laboratories (BSL-2 and chemistry wet labs)
    • 2 lecture auditoriums (seating 160 and 120)
    • A rooftop greenhouse (2,500 sq. ft.)
    • A microscopy imaging core

  • Helena L. Martin Engineering Hall
    Opened: 2005
    Description: Features workshop spaces, prototyping rooms, 12 teaching labs, and a design center supporting civil, electrical, and mechanical engineering programs.

  • Mendell Business Pavilion
    Constructed: 1991
    Highlights:

    • 10 seminar rooms with tiered seating
    • Real-time financial data lab
    • Entrepreneurship incubator suite (capacity: 40 student teams)

Libraries

  • Samuelson Library
    Size: 150,000 volumes, 23,000 sq. ft.
    Facilities:
    • Three floors of print collections & digital media rooms
    • 200 personal study carrels and 24 group study rooms
    • Makerspace (3D printers, VR suites)
    • Café and quiet reading atrium

Residential Life

  • Maple Residence Hall
    Capacity: 385 undergraduate students
    Structure: Eight floors, co-ed, suite-style living with communal kitchens and lounges per level

  • Sequoia Apartments
    Capacity: 164 postgraduates and family housing units (2- and 4-bedroom units)
    Amenities: Onsite laundry, daycare facility, and fitness center

  • Aspen Commons
    Capacity: 190 first-year residents
    Features: Double rooms, learning communities, faculty-in-residence apartment

Dining Facilities

  • The Grove Dining Hall
    Seating: 475
    Description: Central dining area offering all-day buffet, vegan and international stations, and late-night café

  • Market Street Café
    Location: Student Center
    Options: Bistro fare, grab-and-go, espresso bar

Student Life & Recreation

  • Student Union Center
    Size: 48,000 sq. ft.
    Facilities:
    • 400-person auditorium
    • Student organization suites
    • Meeting rooms
    • Student legal services
  • Dalton Fitness & Recreation Complex
    Features:
    • Olympic-size swimming pool
    • Indoor climbing wall
    • Three multicourt gyms
    • Cardio & weight areas
    • Fitness classrooms
    • Outdoor sand volleyball courts

Arts & Performance Spaces

  • Elena Capri Theater
    Capacity: 510-seat auditorium for university and community performances

  • Fine Arts Studios
    Facilities: Drawing, ceramics, printmaking, and digital media labs

Athletics

  • Pinecrest Stadium
    Seating: 4,200
    Primary Uses: Soccer, track and field

  • Varsity Sports Hall
    Facilities: Locker rooms, training suites, and offices for 11 varsity teams

Grounds and Outdoor Spaces

  • Cendara Arboretum (20 acres): Managed collection of native and exotic plants, walking trails, and an outdoor classroom amphitheater.

  • East Lake: Man-made, 3-acre lake with surrounding picnic and study areas.

  • Reflection Garden: Dedicated quiet zone adjacent to Samuelson Library, used for meditation and outdoor study.

  • Bike & Pedestrian Paths: Five miles of interconnected, ADA-accessible trails linking all campus sectors.

Recent and Planned Improvements

  • 2023: Completion of the GreenTech Building (LEED Platinum, houses Environmental Sustainability Programs)
  • 2024: Expansion of outdoor Wi-Fi coverage and replacement of all campus lighting with LED fixtures
  • 2025: Planned construction of the Dr. Maya Singh Health and Wellness Center (mental health, counseling, medical clinic)

Cendara University’s campus infrastructure supports a thriving academic and extracurricular community, prioritizing both technological advancement and sustainability in all future developments.
