Cendara University Financial Aid Guide

Academic Year 2024–2025

The Office of Financial Assistance is dedicated to making a Cendara University education accessible and affordable. Cendara offers a range of scholarships, grants, loans, and work-study opportunities to help offset educational expenses outlined in the annual fee structure. This guide details available aid programs and provides step-by-step application instructions.

1. Types of Financial Aid

Cendara University offers the following forms of assistance:

  • Scholarships: Merit-based, talent-based, and need-based awards that do not require repayment.
  • Grants: Need-based aid, typically from university, state, or federal sources; no repayment required.
  • Loans: Borrowed funds that must be repaid with interest; both federal and institutional loans are available.
  • Work-Study: Part-time campus employment to earn money during the academic year.

Each student may be eligible for a combination of these aid types.

4.2 Institutional Loan Programs (Domestic & International)

  • Cendara Opportunity Loan: Up to $7,500/year (Fixed 6.25% interest, deferred repayment until 6 months post-graduation; cosigner required for international students)
  • Short-term Emergency Loan: Up to $1,200/semester (No interest, payable within 6 months)

Note: Loan counseling is required for all new borrowers.

5. Work-Study Employment

Cendara’s Federal and Institutional Work-Study programs provide part-time, on-campus jobs to assist students in meeting expenses.

  • Typical positions: Library assistant, lab tech, campus events, dining hall, research aide
  • Hourly pay: $12 – $15 (undergraduate), $14 – $18 (graduate)
  • Maximum hours: 15/week during terms

Eligibility depends on need and timely application; indicate work-study interest on the FAFSA or Cendara Application for Aid.

6. Application Procedures

To receive financial aid, applicants must complete the appropriate forms and follow these steps:

Step 1: Gather Required Documents

  • Student and parent tax returns (prior-prior year, e.g., 2022 for 2024–25)
  • W-2s and other income statements
  • Bank statements and records of investments
  • Social Security numbers or governmental IDs (if applicable)
  • Academic transcripts for some merit aid

Step 2: Complete Applications

Aid Type Required Application(s) Link / Submission Method
Federal Aid (U.S.) FAFSA https://studentaid.gov (School Code: 036469)
Need-Based Aid (Intl) CSS Profile https://cssprofile.collegeboard.org
University Scholarships Cendara Scholarship Portal scholarships.cendara.edu/apply
Indiana State Grants FAFSA (by April 15) Same as above
Graduate Department Awards Dept. Application Contact department directly

Step 3: Submit Supplemental Materials

  • Essays, letters of recommendation, or portfolios as specified for certain scholarships
  • Documentation verifying special circumstances (loss of income, medical bills, etc.)
  • International students: proof of English proficiency & financial Documents

Step 4: Meet All Deadlines

  • Priority Deadline: February 15, 2024 (for maximum consideration)
  • Cendara Scholarship Portal: Opens October 1, closes March 1
  • FAFSA/CSS Profile: Opens October 1; final deadline May 1
  • Department/Graduate Scholarships: Vary by unit (consult website)

Step 5: Review Aid Offer & Accept

  • Cendara’s Office of Financial Assistance will send an “Aid Notification” via email/portal.
  • Review your offer, accept/decline individual items online.
  • Submit additional documents if requested (tax forms, clarification letters, etc.)

Step 6: Maintain Eligibility

  • Must be enrolled at least half-time each semester (≥ 12 credits undergraduate, ≥ 9 credits graduate unless otherwise stated)
  • Maintain Satisfactory Academic Progress (minimum GPA 2.0 undergrad/3.0 grad, 67% completion rate)
  • Renew FAFSA or applicable forms each year

7. Additional Financial Support Resources

  • Outside Scholarships: Students encouraged to apply for external awards (employers, civic groups, national competitions).
  • Payment Plans: Flexible semester installment plans available via the Bursar’s Office.
  • Emergency Assistance Fund: One-time support for unforeseen crises; contact deanofstudents@cendara.edu.

8. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I combine multiple scholarships or grants?
A: Yes, but total aid may not exceed direct costs (tuition, fees, room/board). Some awards have restrictions; always check notifications.

Q: I missed the deadline. Can I still apply?
A: Late applications are accepted, but funding may be limited. Contact the Office of Financial Assistance for guidance.

Q: What if my family’s financial situation has recently changed?
A: Submit a Special Circumstances Appeal form with supporting documentation for re-evaluation of your aid package.
